Kiroku · Microsoft 365

OneNote, quietly underneath.

Pacefold keeps the fast daily surface. OneNote becomes the durable notebook behind it. Every capture is saved locally first; the Microsoft connection is optional.

One-time setup

  1. In Microsoft Entra, open Identity → Applications → App registrations → New registration.
  2. Name it Pacefold and choose the organizational account type your OneNote account uses.
  3. For Redirect URI, choose Single-page application (SPA) and enter exactly:
https://rbt4.github.io/pacefold/app/auth.html
  1. Create the registration and copy its Application (client) ID.
  2. Under API permissions, add Microsoft Graph’s delegated Notes.ReadWrite permission.
  3. Do not create a client secret. A browser app cannot keep one confidential.
  4. Return to Pacefold → OneNote, paste the client ID, leave the tenant as organizations unless your administrator supplied another value, and sign in.
  5. Choose the HSSys notebook and the section Pacefold may append to.
Workplace policy still wins. If U of T blocks app registration, user consent, or OneNote Graph access, an administrator must approve it. Captures remain safe locally and queued; Pacefold will not bypass the tenant.

What Pacefold sends

  • One dated page per day in the chosen section.
  • Capture time, category, and text.
  • A OneNote to-do tag for Follow-up captures.

Hydration, prayer, meals, away time, movement resets, and screen-use records are not sent.

How silent sync behaves

New captures queue offline, retry when Pacefold is online, and use a stable capture ID so a retry cannot silently duplicate an item. Disconnecting keeps local captures intact.
